Winter 2015 Quarter Ranked Fight Night

01/16/2015 by Jason Lu

Schedule

Hey guys we were quite pleased with the HUGE turnout for our Ranked Fight Night during Fall so we are excited to announce the event will continue on this quarter as well!

We will still be holding them on Fridays, with signups taking place from 7:30 PM - 9:00 PM. The ranking points from last quarter are also being carried over as well, meaning all of the games from last quarter are returning. The difference now is that we’re introducing two new games to the event series: Ultimate Marvel vs. Capcom 3 and Super Smash Bros. for Nintendo Wii U.

Here’s the schedule:

  1. 01/16: Ultimate Marvel vs. Capcom 3 / Project M
  2. 01/23: Ultra Street Fighter IV / Super Smash Bros. for Nintendo Wii U
  3. 01/30: Ultra Street Fighter IV / Super Smash Bros. Melee
  4. 02/06: Ultimate Marvel vs. Capcom 3 / Project M
  5. 02/13: Ultra Street Fighter IV / Super Smash Bros. for Nintendo Wii U
  6. 02/20: Ultra Street Fighter IV / Super Smash Bros. Melee
  7. 02/27: Ultimate Marvel vs. Capcom 3 / Project M
  8. 03/06: Ultra Street Fighter IV / Super Smash Bros. Melee

To handle having so many games for one event series, we are having one Ranked Fight Night every week now, rather than having the bi-weekly schedule we had last quarter.

Time & Location

We noticed that our old venue was growing more and more cramped as our participants grew, so we are pleased to announce our new venue at Baskin Engineering 2 192 every Friday starting at 7:30 PM. The room is much larger and has a door to the outside for fresh air, but setting up the room for the event will be a bit more complicated now so please bear with us as we try to get used to it.

Season Endings & Prizes

Ultra Street Fighter IV, Super Smash Bros. Melee, and Project M will end their season at the end of this quarter, so prizes will be given to the top 3 players with the highest rank for each game. Special thanks to our sponsor Level Up Video Games for giving us prizes to hand out!

Ultimate Marvel vs. Capcom 3 begins its season this quarter. The turnout depends on whether or not the game continues next quarter, but it will have a definite prize with $50 for 1st place, $10 for 2nd place, and $1 for 3rd place.

Super Smash Bros. for Nintendo Wii U also begins its season this quarter. If the turnout is good enough, it will continue onto next quarter, possibly with prizes given out at the end.

Ultra Street Fighter IV Rules

  • Game Settings: Versus Mode, 99 Seconds, 2/3 Rounds, Edition Select = Off, No Handicap
  • Grand finals, winners finals, and losers finals will be best of 5 per set. All other matches will be best of 3.
  • If the players do not agree on a stage within 15 seconds, the match will be random stage select.
  • Winner may change ultra only if opponent changes characters, but has to pick ultra first after loser picks character.

Super Smash Bros. Melee Rules

  • Game Settings: 4 Stock, 8 Minute Time Limit, No Items
  • Grand finals, winners finals, and losers finals will be best of 5 per set. All other matches will be best of 3.
  • Starter stages: Yoshi’s Story, Fountain of Dreams, Final Destination, Battlefield, Dream Land
  • Additional counter-pick Stages: Pokemon Stadium
  • Wobbling is legal. Freezing and infinite stalling are not legal.
  • First stage is decided by banning from the Starter Stages in P1-P2-P2-P1 order.
  • Winning player is not locked into the same character for the next match, but has to pick before loser selects character.
  • Following stages decided by winner banning one stage from all stages and then loser choosing.
  • Any matches that best of 5, there are no stage bans.
  • You may not choose a stage you have already won on unless mutually agreed to.

Project M Rules

  • Game Settings: 4 Stock, 8 Minute Time Limit, No Items
  • Grand finals, winners finals, and losers finals will be best of 5 per set. All other matches will be best of 3.
  • Starter stages: Yoshi’s Story, Fountain of Dreams, Final Destination, Battlefield, Dream Land, Pokemon Stadium 2, Smashville
  • Additional counter-pick Stages: Yoshi's Island, WarioWare, Norfair, Skyworld, Green Hill Zone
  • Freezing and infinite stalling are not legal.
  • First stage is decided by banning from the Starter Stages in P1-P2-P1 order.
  • Winning player is not locked into the same character for the next match, but has to pick before loser selects character.
  • Following stages decided by winner banning three stages from all legal stages and then loser choosing from the remaining available stages.
  • Any matches that best of 5, there are no stage bans.
  • You may not choose a stage you have already won on unless mutually agreed to.
